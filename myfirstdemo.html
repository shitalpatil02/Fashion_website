The ‘Emily in Paris’ Outfits Are as Iconic as You’d Expect

  • Emily’s morning after outfit – messy bun, heels, boyfriend blazer and Balmain skirt – was très chic, but her Giambattista Valli x H&M tulle gown stole the show
  • From her crystal-studded Prada bag à la 1920s to the colourful Dolce & Gabbana crossbody piece, even Emily’s accessories aren’t exempt from her love for bold patterns


She experiments with multiple patterns and colours in one of her midseason looks

Yellow blazer, a checkered tank top, a multicoloured skirt and lavender knee-high boots. To complete the outfit, she carries a small Dolce & Gabbana crossbody bag.




Her heart-patterned dress looks like it’s from a different era

Emily steals the show in the mid-length heart-print dress, which was created by Anouki, a Georgia-based designer.

Field considered removing the detachable puffy sleeves so Emily’s coat would fit over the dress, but Fitoussi solved the problem by designing and creating the red overcoat she wears in just two days.




The red tulle gown Emily wears to the fashion show at Versailles is one of her all-time best looks

High-low gown from the Giambattista Valli x H&M 2019 collaboration.

Aside from the dress’ deep-V neckline and flowing train, the outfit details – from the hair embellishments to the heart necklace.




Emily uses green as a through-thread in an otherwise busy outfit

Versace polo shirt and a Mugler skirt with strappy multicoloured heels.




Her high-low sundress is perfect for the sun-drenched south of France

The dress is designed by Magali Pascal as part of the brand’s spring/summer 2021 collection, per Grazia magazine.

The exaggerated ruffles, the open back and the high low skirt were an expression of celebration, returning to finding joy in dressing again.
